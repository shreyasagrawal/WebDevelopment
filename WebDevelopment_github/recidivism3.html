Part 3: Mapping Police Precincts

In the 2013 US District Court case referenced in Part 2, the NYPD had defended their stop-and-frisk practices, saying that since crimes mostly happen in black neighborhoods, it is natural that more black people would be found suspicious of crimes (New York City Bar Association 2013, p. 10). We can evaluate this statement by comparing the racial distribution of each police precinct to the number of stops and arrests.

Figure 3A: This shows the distribution of African-Americans in New York City by precint. Figure 3B: Color coded map that shows the number of arrests per 1000 African-Americans in New York City in each precinct.

In Figure 3A we can see that the precincts with darker colors tend to be in the southeast part of the city. This tells us there are a higher number of African-Americans that live in those southeast precincts. In Figure 3B we see precincts with the darkest colors, representing the likelihood of being arrested, tends to be on the northwest section of the city. Through this graph we see that Black people are much less likely to be arrested in areas with higher African-American populations.

KEY IDEA: A graph in itself cannot prove that racial discrimination causes the disparity in arrest data. However, a careful examination of these graphs does lead us to discredit the argument that the racial distribution of the precincts caused the disparity in arrests.

Explore the data:

Use the app below to look at multiple graphics evaulating NYPD stops and arrests based upon year, race, gender, and type of crime.

To make a graph that looks like Figure 3A, select:
  • Color Precincts by:   Racial distribution of City: Black
  • Remove Precints:   22
  • Scale:   Logarithmic
  • Choose all years:   (2006-2018)
  • Facet By:   Year 
  • Color By:   None

    • To better evaluate more recent patterns in racial disparities of the NYPD stops, change "Choose all years" to (2014-2018)

    Additional Questions to Consider:

  • Add precinct 22 back into the dataset, then click on that precinct in the MAP. Notice that Precinct 22 tends to have very different characteristics than most other precincts and is often removed from the data as an outlier. Zoom into the graph and provide a brief explanation as to why Precinct 22 may be different than other locations.

  • Precinct 114 is close to the center of the city and tends to have a large number of arrests. Zoom into this location and identify a feature of this precinct that could explain the large number of arrests in this area. (hint: what is Rikers Island?)
